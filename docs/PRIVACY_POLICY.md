# Privacy Policy for Rulebook

**Last Updated: April 2, 2026**

## Introduction

Rulebook ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application.

## Information We Collect

### Information You Provide
- **Rules and Habits**: The personal rules, habits, and goals you create within the app
- **Categories**: Custom categories you create to organize your rules
- **Check-in Data**: Your daily check-ins and compliance tracking
- **Notes and Reflections**: Any notes or reflections you add to your rules

### Automatically Collected Information
- **Usage Data**: Information about how you use the app (e.g., features accessed, time spent)
- **Device Information**: Device type, operating system version, and app version
- **Local Storage**: All data is stored locally on your device using iOS standard storage

## How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:
- Provide and maintain the Rulebook app functionality
- Analyze usage patterns to improve the app
- Send you notifications and reminders (if you enable them)
- Provide customer support

## Data Storage and Security

- **Local Storage**: All your personal data (rules, habits, check-ins) is stored locally on your device
- **No Cloud Sync**: We do not transmit your personal data to our servers
- **iCloud Backup**: If you have iCloud backup enabled, your data may be backed up to your personal iCloud account
- **Security**: We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data

## Data Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or share your personal information with third parties. Your data remains on your device.

## Your Rights

You have the right to:
- Access your data at any time through the app
- Delete your data by deleting the app
- Export your data (if export functionality is available)
- Opt-out of notifications through iOS settings

## Children's Privacy

Rulebook is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13.
